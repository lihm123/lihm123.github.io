About FingerTip

FingerTip contains over 20,000 episodes, covering demonstrations of various daily tasks performed by humans on more than 500 different Android apps, derived from scenarios in users' daily lives, which includes user-related contextual information and users’ long-term mobile phone usage patterns. FingerTip also includes two brand-new tracks, to evaluate the ability of mobile GUI agents to proactively predict user intents and offer suggestions, as well as their ability to personalize task execution in accordance with user preferences.

Overview of FingerTip

We construct FingerTip using real-world human demonstrations of multi-step Android interactions, continuously collected from users' daily lives with essential contextual information. Based on this, we design a novel dataset and introduce two innovative tracks: proactive task suggestions and personalized task execution. You may check our paper for more details.

Contribute to FingerTip

We invite you to advance mobile interaction research by training novel models using the FingerTip dataset. By expanding the range of models benchmarked on our data, we can enhance understanding of task automation and discover more effective approaches. If you develop innovative architectures or training methodologies, we welcome your contributions to build a more comprehensive foundation for proactive and personalized mobile agents.
